# Stage 3 — Product analysis

Generate `./planmysaas-blueprint/03-analysis.md`. Read `01-idea.md` and `02-research.md` first.

## Output structure

```markdown
# Product analysis — <Project name>

## Executive summary
3-4 sentences. Verdict on the idea. Strongest signal. Biggest blocker. One sentence on what to do this week.

## Overall PMF score · /100
Single number + one-line label (e.g. "62 — Promising demand, weak focus").

## SWOT
Four lists, 4-6 items each:
- **Strengths** — what the product naturally has
- **Weaknesses** — internal blockers
- **Opportunities** — external tailwinds
- **Threats** — substitutes, competitors, regulation, AI commoditisation

## TAM / SAM / SOM
Directional estimates only. For each: range + 1-line basis. Mark confidence as "low / medium / high".

## Porter's 5 Forces
Each force: score 1-10 + label (Low/Med/High) + 1-sentence reasoning.
- Competitive rivalry
- Threat of new entrants
- Bargaining power of buyers
- Bargaining power of suppliers
- Threat of substitutes

## Business Model Canvas (9 blocks)

For each block, 3-5 items:
1. **Value Propositions**
2. **Customer Segments**
3. **Channels**
4. **Customer Relationships**
5. **Revenue Streams**
6. **Key Resources**
7. **Key Activities**
8. **Key Partnerships**
9. **Cost Structure**

## Competitive positioning

- **Position statement** — one sentence of where you stand vs everyone else
- **Current strengths** (4 bullets)
- **Near-term differentiators** (4 bullets — things you can ship in 90 days)
- **Future moat candidates** (3 bullets — what compounds over years)
- **Vulnerabilities** (4 bullets — honest weaknesses competitors will exploit)

## Risk matrix (5-7 risks)

For each: risk name · category (Execution / Market / Tech / Financial / Regulatory) · likelihood (1-10) · impact (1-10) · 1-line reasoning · 1-line mitigation.

## PMF score breakdown (6 dimensions)

Each dimension scored 0-100 + reasoning:
- Problem Clarity
- Solution Fit
- Market Size
- Willingness to Pay
- Competitive Advantage
- Execution Readiness

## Strategic recommendations (top 8)

Phased: build-now (4) / validate-next (3) / avoid (1). Each: priority (Critical/High/Medium) · recommendation · rationale · timeframe.

## Go-to-market strategy

- **Launch approach** (1 paragraph)
- **Target segment** (most narrow possible)
- **Acquisition channels** (top 5)
- **Pricing strategy** (1 paragraph with numbers)
- **Key metrics** (top 5 to track)
- **Expansion discipline** (1 paragraph — what NOT to do)
```

## Quality rules

- The PMF score must be honest. If the idea has weak signal, score it 40-50 and explain. Founders need the truth.
- Risk matrix's "mitigation" column must be actionable in <30 days.
- Recommendations must be sharp — "narrow to college students" beats "consider focusing".

## Tone

Senior advisor reviewing a pitch. Direct. No hedging beyond confidence labels.
